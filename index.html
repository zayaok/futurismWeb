About

What is Afrofuturism? Wikipedia describes it as :

Afrofuturism is a cultural aesthetic, philosophy of science and philosophy of history that combines elements of science fiction, historical fiction, fantasy, Afrocentrism, and magic realism with non-Western cosmologies in order to critique not only the present-day dilemmas of black people, but also to revise, interrogate, and re-examine the historical events of the past.

Afrofuturism uses surreal and otherwordly enviroments to alter the way people think of black people,in reality. Afrofuturistic writers and artists push the boundaries of what is acceptable and what is necesscary. While the term was coined in the early 90's, the works presented go all the way back to the 1950's. Many of the themes and concerns of the African diaspora are often dealt with through technoculture and science fiction.

Who is it for

My introduction to the this beautiful art form was finding a dusty, hardcover book by Octavia Butler in my grandmother's basement called "Lilith's Brood". I call this my intro but I'd long been around the works of arts by "The Funkadelics" or "Parliment", another generational gift courtesy of my grandmother. Since discovering this genre, it validated and described my life and experiences; although in space or in a new world. When I was younger, I considered it my escape. A world where black women could do anything, be steely-eyed captains, mad inventors or androids questioning what it meant to be human. I believe afrofuturism is for everyone, who envisions a future where ANYONE can be ANYTHING.

Zalika Ockleberry
